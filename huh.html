Aleksei Leonov, Russian cosmonaut and first person to conduct a spacewalk



Return Home

I think I have a mechanical bull instead of a heart. It creaks and rattles. I can’t stay upright.
I need to find a train, an iron horse
Where I can feel its beating heart, linked to my own
Like brothers swapping blood
Incinerator engine, swallowing coal.

Larger than life, rusted, wiry, and heavy. The tracks might eat me if I’m not careful.
I’d leave my car by the crossing, and I’d remember it with tears in my eyes.

Rumbling roaring
Against fingertips
And Shaking Palms

I’m in love with some
Those wider than my view, out of my perspective
I collect nails in my heels.

A monster of metal, almost ancestral
Carrying me under glittering stars, re-introducing us every night.

I’m a contemporary cowboy; desperately afraid of taking the reins
Maybe I’m pathetic

My boots succumb to dry rot
My memory too.

Can you carry me across a country?

I don’t want a boyfriend and I don’t want a girlfriend
I want the angel that hangs above The Lovers tattooed on my lower back
Or the base of my neck.
Or between my breasts.

I might be hopeless, I might be a romantic

I read Shakespeare in high school and never cared for it
Only the frilled collar.
Or the theatrics of a stage
Maybe that’s all I’m meant to be.

I forgot how to play the piano and my fingers weather from regret

Me?
I don’t care much.

Humming

Who has faith in me? I have a need to be mythic

Like the beads of sand– of tenderness– and impossibility:
Falling through clenched fists
    Like lies told by a roadside palm reader
