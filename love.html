Prayer to all the beds I've slept in that weren't mine

Written for Jacob's final for a class I wasn't in that I never got to see
shrug
I remember your smell, how you felt across my skin
Grant me grace
Grant me kindness, your glimmers of sincerity 

I pray to the beds I’ve slept in, 
Beds that aren’t mine– flickering comfort and brief, whole, translucent understanding

To be let in, to be vulnerable, how kind. 
Under slotted moonlight, maybe besides a bedside lamp
Or a cat tree, or maybe nothing at all. 

Whispering to me,
	Like I’d wish the stars would
Answering me,
	Like I’d wish Capital G God would 

Let me float, face down, in the lake of your cotton
	One last time (again and again)
Though, this time I may not make it to shore.

I pray I find you again, over and over
Maybe the scent will be different, maybe I’ll retch
An experience just as revered

You’re much realer to me than the cosmos
Or the depths of the sea

How intense is this need
This yearning to be held, felt, and seen
By the unanimity that is someone else’s bed. 

May each bed grant another page turning
Another line of mine read, alongside another instance of my dreaded truth 
Of Softly brushing our teeth in well loved pajamas, side by side
Or of drunkenly stripping one another 
Or of softly weeping

May I never stop leaving another bed in another room with the memory of my presence 
Allow me to forever stamp the routines of others with my outline
May I never stop the search for theirs in mine

You Are Here!

The stars hold hands and they might’ve already died
They might have burned up before we were tall
When we lived across the country, under the same moon and stars
Parallel, unknowing lives

Maybe we spied on the same constellations
Tumbled and giggled under the same summer skies
Watching the sun trail across the same path, three hours apart
Maybe we’d innocently hold hands, just like our dying stars
Unaware of the same gods, dancing under our light
Small and innocent
With missing teeth, collecting rolly pollies, on bandaged knees

When friendship simply meant knowing the same words
And living on the same street

How lucky I am to have met you
How lucky I have been to have known you
How cruel the stars are, for not introducing us sooner
How cruel of the stars, for not letting us stick
Like the drying rolly pollies, to the airtight tupperware I housed them in
Unknowing of living breath.

Happy Tail Syndrome

In my dreams I marry. Somewhere in the mountains, no wedding, just a ceremony. You love me kindly as you naturally always have. I do everything in my power to return the beautiful kindness. In my dreams it works. We’re truthful and headstrong, smart and wonderful. You read about the world, its histories and its futures, I talk about painting. I’m not sure if I do. I’m forever in awe of you. Like a dog of its man.
